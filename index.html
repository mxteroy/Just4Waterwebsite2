An Aggie student non-profit organization dedicated to providing self-sustainable and economic solutions to communities with the most need around the globe.

Our Mission

To help alleviate the current water scarcity crisis by enabling impoverished communities worldwide with tools necessary to create self-sustainable and affordable water solutions.

Our Vision

To be a leading non-profit organization with worldwide chapters supporting innovative solutions and inspiring people to be key players in the fight for global access to clean water.

The Beginning

Just4Water grew from the aspirations of two Texas A&M college students, Guillermo Gomez-Salas and Mark Membreño, who had a passion for helping developing countries reduce the issue of water scarcity. With the support of other college students and professors, J4W was founded in January 2014 and built their first water well in Paso Real, Nicaragua on March 20, 2014.
